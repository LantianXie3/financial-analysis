## Why Ratio Analysis Matters

Raw financial statements — the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement — tell us *what* happened. Ratio analysis tells us *what it means*.

By translating raw figures into standardized metrics, ratio analysis allows us to:

- **Benchmark performance** against industry peers and historical trends, independent of company size.
- **Surface warning signals** in liquidity or leverage that may not be obvious in aggregate totals.
- **Separate operational efficiency from capital structure decisions**, giving leadership a cleaner view of where value is being created or destroyed.
- **Track momentum** — whether margins are expanding, asset turns improving, or debt rising faster than earnings.

In short, ratios compress complex financial data into the signals that actually drive strategic decisions. They are the vocabulary of financial health.

---

## Six Ratio Categories

The analysis will be organized across six standard categories:

| Category | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Performance** | How effectively the company converts revenue into earnings (e.g., EPS, revenue growth) |
| **Profitability** | Margin quality across gross, operating, and net income levels |
| **Efficiency** | How productively the company deploys its assets and manages its working capital |
| **Leverage** | The degree to which the company relies on debt versus equity to finance operations |
| **Liquidity** | The company's ability to meet near-term obligations without disruption |
| **Du Pont Analysis** | A decomposition of Return on Equity into its three constituent drivers: margin, asset turnover, and financial leverage |

All figures will reflect the most recent two fiscal years to enable year-over-year comparison.
